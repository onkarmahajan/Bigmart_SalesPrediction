Bigmart, a one-stop-shopping-center, has been discussed to predict the sales of different types of items and for understanding the effects of different factors on the itemsâ€™ sales.


Problem statement

The data scientists at Bigmart have collected 2013 sales data for 1559 products across 10 stores in different cities. Also, certain attributes of each product and store have been defined. The aim is to build a predictive model and find out the sales of each product at a particular store.

There are a total of 12 features in the Dataset, out of which some are altered to fit in the machine learning model.
See Data Exploration for more information.


After performing Data Exploration and Data Cleaning, we are left with 10 input features which are as follows:

Weight: Weight of the Item in grams.
Visibility: How well the item is visible in the store (range: 0 - 1).
Max Retail Price: MRP of the item.
Outlet Years: How much old is the outlet.
Fat: Fat content of the item (Low Fat, Regular, Non-Edible).
Item Type: Food, drink or Non-Consumable (NC).
Outlet: Which outlet you are referring to.
Outlet Size: Size of the outlet (High, medium or small)
Tier: Tier of the city the outlet is located in (Tier 1, tier 2 or tier 3).
Outlet Type: What type of outlet it is (Grocery store, supermarket type1, or supermarket type2)


Now, the last remaining input is the machine learning model. Currently you can choose from 3 models i.e. Linear Regression, Decision Tree Regression and Random Forest Regression.
The output of each model will be the Outlet_Sales and it's respective accuracy scores. With this you can judge how well this model is fitted to our Dataset.
